Youth Health Ambassador Program (YHAP)

About the Program

The Youth Health Ambassador Programme (YHAP) is a strategic joint initiative of the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MOHFW) and UNICEF. The programme is designed to empower youth by enhancing their health awareness and building their capacity in primary prevention and health promotion thereby equipping them to serve as informed health ambassador.

These programs equip youth with knowledge and skills in areas like physical health including SRH and mental wellbeing, enabling them to become active advocates for health and influence healthier choices within their communities and networks.

Definitions

Youth

As per United Nations, youth refers to those persons aged between the ages of 15 and 24 without prejudice to other definitions by Member States.

Young People

Covers the age range 10-24 years.

Adolescents

Individuals in the 10-19 years age group.

Health (WHO Definition)

A state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being, and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.

Core Components of the YHAP

Our program is built on six fundamental pillars that empower youth to become effective health ambassadors

Health Literacy

YHAP builds foundational health literacy through comprehensive training on essential health and wellbeing. This equips ambassadors with the expertise to act as credible sources of information and effective advocates for prevention and promotion.

Health Education and Awareness

Youth Health Ambassadors (YHAs) will create health education and awareness through campaigns on key health issues, comprehensive trainings, mentorships etc., Through these multifaceted efforts, they will ensure the accurate dissemination of crucial health information, ultimately fostering well-informed and health-literate communities.

Peer to Peer Influence

The programme is built on the principle that peer-to-peer engagement is a powerful catalyst for change. By facilitating supportive mentorship and encouraging positive role-modeling among contemporaries, YHAP leverages the profound impact of shared experiences to promote healthy behaviors.

Empowerment

YHAP empowers individuals by equipping them with the tools, confidence, and skills needed to take effective control and contribute to the economic development, creating a productive, resilient, and healthy workforce for the future.

Leadership

The program cultivates leadership qualities in youth, preparing them to become effective, ethical, and inspiring agents of change in their communities.

Advocacy

The Youth Health Ambassador Programme (YHAP) builds foundational competencies in health advocacy, empowering youth to effectively raise voice, articulate public health priorities to drive systemic reform. This is achieved through strategic engagement with key stakeholders, evidence-based promotion of policies, and active contribution to the formulation of legislation for strengthening health systems.

Critical Development Phase

Adolescence is a crucial period of physical, mental, and social development that shapes lifelong health patterns.

Large Population

Young people represent a significant portion of the population, making their health a priority for community wellbeing.

Prevention is Key

Early intervention and health education can prevent many chronic diseases and unhealthy behaviors in adulthood.

Mental Health Matters

Adolescence is when many mental health conditions emerge, requiring awareness and early support.

Economic Impact

Healthy youth become productive adults, contributing to economic development and societal progress.

Global Health Goals

Adolescent health is integral to achieving Sustainable Development Goals and universal health coverage.
